# 6. 調理器具のパラドックス

![ice](./images/6-ice.png)

Hi Sam, I’ve got something intriguing to share with you! You won't believe what I'm looking at.

It's an extraordinary situation where I'm in a futuristic kitchen, and I've come across a sink that operates in a bizarre way. Instead of liquid water, there's a continuous stream of perfectly shaped ice cubes flowing from the faucet.

Shaped like a sleek, modern swan-neck tap, the faucet is made of entirely transparent glass. Interestingly, you can see the small ice cubes moving through the neck before they drop into the basin. The sink itself is also made of clear glass, and it's currently half-filled with these frozen cubes. What’s fascinating is that the ice cubes seem to be attached to each other like a crystal chain as they pour out, yet they remain individual squares. It seems to be some kind of advanced cooling system that eliminates the need for a freezer tray.

It's a stunning piece of design, but I'm not sure how I'd wash my hands with it! Would you like to do this someday? Having an endless supply of ice directly from the tap would be great for parties.
