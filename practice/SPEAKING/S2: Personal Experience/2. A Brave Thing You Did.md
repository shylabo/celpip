# Question

Talk about something you did that you think was brave. What happened, why do you think it was brave, and how did you feel about it?

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Considering my personal history, the time I stood up to a hostile manager at my old job stands out as truly extraordinary.

Primarily, the aspect of confronting him during a busy staff meeting is what makes this memory so vivid.

One of my colleagues was being treated atrociously, and I knew I couldn’t just stand around and watch. I decided to bite the bullet and point out his unfair treatment in front of the whole team.

Given that he was in a high position, it was a formidable challenge for me at the time.

Concerning my emotions during that moment, I was initially filled with dread, and my hands were even trembling. However, after I spoke up, the manager actually apologized, and I felt a sense of jubilation and relief.

Undoubtedly, this experience remains a magnificent part of my past.
