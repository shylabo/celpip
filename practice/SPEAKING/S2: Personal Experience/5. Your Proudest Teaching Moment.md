# Question

Talk about an experience where you taught something to someone else. Maybe you helped a classmate understand a difficult subject, showed a family member how to use new technology, or taught a skill to a child. What did you teach, how did you teach it, and why was this teaching experience particularly memorable for you?

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Considering my personal history, teaching my elderly grandmother how to use video calling stands out as truly extraordinary.

Primarily, the aspect of showing her how to access the applications and initiate a call is what makes this memory so vivid.

Initially, she was filled with a sense of dread about potentially breaking the device, so I had to be very patient and look over the steps with her multiple times. We had to hash out the basic functions for an hour until she finally managed to figure it out on her own.

Concerning why this was so memorable, seeing her face light up when she finally connected with her sister abroad was astounding. It was integral for her to feel connected to her family, and once she got the hang of it, she realized the process was actually a piece of cake.

Undoubtedly, this experience remains a magnificent part of my past
