# Question

Do you think children should start learning a second language in elementary school or wait until they are teenagers in high school? Explain your reasons.

Recording time: 90s

# Answer

The dialogue surrounding the topic of whether children should initiate learning a second language in elementary school is very thought-provoking, and I am firmly persuaded that starting at a younger age is the most beneficial approach.
To begin with, younger children have a unique cognitive capacity to absorb linguistic patterns naturally. Given that their brains are highly adaptable, they can attain native-like pronunciation and fluency much more swiftly than teenagers. This early exposure is valuable because it allows them to develop a strong foundation without the rigorous struggle often faced when starting later in life.
Furthermore, starting in elementary school provides students with ample time to prosper and master the language before they reach the high-pressure environment of high school. Rather than waiting until their schedules are engrossed with complex academic subjects, they can stay on the ball by building their communication skills early. Indeed, this long-term exposure ensures they can afford the time needed to reach a professional level of proficiency.
Additionally, early language learning unveils new cultures and perspectives, which is integral to fostering global citizenship. Undoubtedly, being bilingual provides an extraordinary advantage in today’s interconnected world, making future career opportunities a piece of cake.
In conclusion, it’s indisputably evident that introducing a second language in elementary school would culminate in a more proficient, culturally aware, and successful generation.
