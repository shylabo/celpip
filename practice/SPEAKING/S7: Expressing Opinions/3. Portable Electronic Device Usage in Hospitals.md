# Question

Do you think hospitals should completely ban the use of personal electronic devices (like smartphones and tablets) in patient care areas? Explain your reasons.

Recording time: 90s

# Answer

The dialogue surrounding the topic of whether hospitals should completely ban personal electronic devices in patient care areas is very thought-provoking, and I am firmly persuaded that a total ban would be more harmful than beneficial.
To begin with, maintaining a connection with the outside world is vital for a patient’s emotional well-being. Given that hospital stays often involve significant trauma, dread, and anxiety, being able to communicate with family members is substantial for recovery. Rather than letting patients suffer in isolation, these devices provide a tremendous sense of comfort and support.
Furthermore, the use of tablets and smartphones allows patients to stay on the ball with their personal responsibilities or simply kick back and relax. Hospital environments can often be chaotic or produce extreme fatigue, and having a distraction can be a superb way to manage stress. Provided that patients are respectful of noise levels, these devices are valuable tools for passing the time.
Additionally, while there are concerns about interference with equipment, modern technology has made such risks minimal. Hospitals should focus on maintaining sanitary conditions for these devices rather than implementing a coercive or shortsighted total ban. A ban would likely lead to utter frustration among patients and their families.
In conclusion, it’s indisputably evident that personal electronic devices are integral to the modern patient experience, and a complete ban would only culminate in unnecessary stress and isolation.
