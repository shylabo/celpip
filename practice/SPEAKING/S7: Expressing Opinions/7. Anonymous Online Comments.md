# Question

Do you think websites and social media platforms should require users to use their real names when leaving comments, or should anonymous commenting be allowed? Explain your reasons.

Recording time: 90s

# Answer

The dialogue surrounding the topic of whether social media platforms should require users to use their real names is very thought-provoking, and I am firmly persuaded that this requirement is vital for maintaining a healthy and respectful digital environment.
To begin with, requiring real identities ensures that an individual’s reputation is at stake whenever they choose to share their opinions. Given that anonymity often acts as an enabler for users to bash or disparage others without consequence, using real names establishes a more civilized norm for online behavior. When people cannot hide behind an obscure or anonymous profile, they are much more likely to stay on the ball and remain professional.
Moreover, this policy would significantly reduce the volume of mendacious information and dubious claims that currently cause utter confusion in our society. If commenters are made culpable for the content they post, they will be prompted to be more discreet and coherent in their arguments rather than acting on impulsive urges to start a digital debacle. This shift is substantial for ensuring that the information we consume is authentic.
Additionally, from an ethical perspective, it allows platforms to moderate conversations more effectively and protect vulnerable users from nefarious activities. Provided that everyone signs in with their true identity, the internet becomes a more unambiguous and safe space for a superb exchange of ideas.
In conclusion, it’s indisputably evident that requiring real names will culminate in a more responsible, honest, and exceptional online community for everyone.
