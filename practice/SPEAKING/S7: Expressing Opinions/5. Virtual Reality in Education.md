# Question

Do you think schools should incorporate virtual reality technology as a regular teaching tool in classrooms? Explain your reasons.

Recording time: 90s

# Answer

The dialogue surrounding the topic of whether schools should incorporate virtual reality (VR) technology as a regular teaching tool is very thought-provoking, and I am firmly persuaded that this would be an extraordinary advancement for modern education.
To begin with, VR provides an immersive learning environment that prevents students from spacing out during mononotonous lectures. Rather than simply reading about history or science in a textbook, students can simulate complex environments, such as ancient civilizations or the deep ocean. This makes the learning experience more authentic and engaging for everyone involved.
Furthermore, this technology is vital for mastering rigorous subjects like anatomy or engineering. Given that many students struggle to grasp abstract concepts through traditional methods, being able to look over and interact with 3D models provides a substantial advantage. Provided that teachers are well-trained to supervise these sessions, students will be able to attain a much deeper level of understanding swiftly.
Additionally, VR serves as a superb alternative to expensive field trips, which are often scarce due to budget cuts. It allows students to venture off to distant parts of the universe without leaving their desks, ensuring that educational opportunities are eligible for all.
