# Question

Do you think parents should have the right to monitor and control their children's internet activities until they turn 18? Consider aspects like privacy, safety, and personal development in your response. Explain your reasons.

Recording time: 90s

# Question

The dialogue surrounding the topic of whether parents should monitor and control their children's internet activities until they turn 18 is very thought-provoking, and I am firmly persuaded that such oversight is a vital responsibility to ensure a minor's safety and well-being.
To begin with, the internet is unfortunately home to various detrimental and untrustworthy elements. Given that children and many teenagers are not yet mature enough to grasp the long-term consequences of their digital footprint, it is substantial for parents to watch out for predatory behavior or cyberbullying. Rather than leaving them vulnerable to a chaotic digital environment, monitoring acts as a necessary safety net.
Furthermore, unrestricted access can often lead to a monotonous lifestyle where children become engrossed in dull or addictive content. Many minors might try to sneak into restricted websites or sneak out of their sleep schedules to stay online. Now that digital consumption is at an all-time high, parental control is beneficial to prioritize educational usage and ensure that internet time does not hinder their personal development.
Additionally, although critics argue about privacy, the ethical duty of a parent to protect their child remains paramount. Provided that families maintain an open line of communication, this monitoring can be supportive and truthful rather than deceitful. It teaches children how to stay on the ball regarding their own digital security in the future.
In conclusion, it’s indisputably evident that allowing parents to monitor internet activities is integral to raising a responsible and safe generation in our modern era.
