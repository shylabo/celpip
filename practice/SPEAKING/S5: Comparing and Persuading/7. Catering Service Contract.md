# Question

Your company is hosting a series of monthly lunch meetings for staff training, and you need to select a catering service. You find two suitable options. Choose the option that you prefer. In the next section, you will need to persuade your manager that your choice is the better choice.

1. Fresh Daily Catering
   • $15 per person
   • variety of hot and cold dishes
   • includes vegetarian options
   • provides plates and utensils
   • minimum order: 20 people

2. Green Earth Kitchen
   • $18 per person
   • organic ingredients
   • accommodates all dietary restrictions
   • eco-friendly packaging
   • minimum order: 15 people

Your manager is suggesting another catering service. Persuade your manager that the service you chose is more suitable by comparing the two.

Manager's Suggestion: Quick Bites Catering
• $12 per person
• limited menu options
• sandwiches and salads only
• no accommodation for dietary restrictions
• minimum order: 30 people

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Hi [Manager's Name], glad to talk to you again! Deciding between these catering services is certainly a tough choice; nevertheless, it’s clear that Green Earth Kitchen provides a more advantageous option for our training sessions.
First and foremost, being able to accommodate all dietary restrictions is vital to ensure every staff member feels included. Given that your suggested "Quick Bites" service has a limited menu and no dietary accommodation, it could lead to utter disappointment or leave some employees hungry. We really shouldn't hold off on providing inclusive options if we want the team to stay focused.
Furthermore, although Quick Bites is less pricey, their minimum order of thirty people is quite a formidable hurdle. Provided that we choose Green Earth, the lower minimum of fifteen allows us to be much more efficient with our budget when smaller groups are training. If we stay on the ball with these details, I am confident our monthly meetings will prosper.
