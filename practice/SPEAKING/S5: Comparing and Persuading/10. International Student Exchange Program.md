# Question

You are a student advisor at a university, and you need to recommend an international exchange program for incoming students. You find two suitable options. Choose the option that you prefer. In the next section, you will need to persuade a student's parent that your choice is the better choice.

1. European University Program
   • 4-month semester program
   • courses taught in English
   • homestay accommodation included
   • $8,000 including accommodation and meals
   • cultural excursions every weekend

2. Asian University Program
   • 6-month semester program
   • language learning support provided
   • dormitory accommodation
   • $6,000 including accommodation
   • weekly cultural workshops

A student's parent is suggesting another exchange program. Persuade them that the program you chose is more suitable by comparing the two.

Parent's Suggestion: Online International Program
• 3-month virtual exchange
• recorded lectures
• virtual cultural activities
• $2,000 program fee
• no travel required

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

First and foremost, the physical experience of traveling and living abroad is vital for personal growth. Given that the online program consists of recorded lectures and virtual activities, it simply cannot rival the real-world interactions found in a four-month immersion program [36, 58, Source]. We shouldn't hold off on giving your child this extraordinary opportunity to develop independence and global perspective [3, 13, Source].
Furthermore, although the virtual option is less pricey, it lacks the stellar cultural excursions and homestay environment included in the European choice [76, 79, Source]. Provided that they are fully immersed in the local culture every weekend, the educational outcomes will be far more valuable than any virtual activity [59, 25, Source]. Choosing this path ensures they stay on the ball with their studies while gaining a superb cultural education [76, Source].
