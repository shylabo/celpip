# Question

You are a fitness coordinator at a community center, and you need to implement a new fitness program for senior citizens. You find two suitable options. Choose the option that you prefer. In the next section, you will need to persuade the community center director that your choice is the better choice.

1. Gentle Movement Classes
   • 45-minute sessions, three times per week
   • focuses on flexibility and balance
   • includes chair exercises and light stretching
   • $75 per month for members
   • maximum 15 participants per class

2. Water Aerobics Program
   • 60-minute sessions, twice per week
   • low-impact exercises in heated pool
   • includes resistance training
   • $90 per month for members
   • maximum 12 participants per class

The community center director is suggesting another program. Persuade the director that the program you chose is more suitable by comparing the two.

Director's Suggestion: Walking Club
• 30-minute walks, five times per week
• outdoor activity (weather dependent)
• no professional instruction
• free for members
• unlimited participants

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Hi there Director, glad to talk to you again! Deciding between these senior fitness programs is certainly a tough choice; nevertheless, it’s clear that the Gentle Movement Classes provide a more advantageous option for our community center.
First and foremost, professional instruction is vital for this age group. Given that your suggested Walking Club lacks expert guidance, it could lead to a formidable safety risk if a participant were to venture off or lose their balance. Our classes focus on flexibility, which is integral to preventing abysmal accidents.
Furthermore, although the Walking Club is free, being an outdoor activity makes it abruptly dependent on the weather. It would be utter chaos to postpone sessions constantly due to rain or heat. Provided that we host these classes indoors, our seniors can keep up with a rigorous routine and truly prosper.
Do you think my points make sense? Let me know your thoughts!
