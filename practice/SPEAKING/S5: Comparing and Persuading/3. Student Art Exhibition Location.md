# Question

You are part of a student art committee organizing an end-of-year exhibition for student artwork. You have found two possible venues. Choose the option that you prefer. In the next section, you will need to persuade the committee president that your choice is the better choice.

1. City Art Gallery
   • free exhibition space for student shows
   • professional lighting and display walls
   • open daily 10am-6pm
   • limited to 2-week exhibition period

2. University Main Hall
   • $200 rental fee
   • large open space with natural lighting
   • flexible exhibition duration (up to 4 weeks)
   • open 24/7 for students and staff

The committee president is suggesting another venue. Persuade them that the venue you chose is more suitable by comparing the two.

Committee President's Choice: Local Community Center
• $150 rental fee
• basic fluorescent lighting
• only open weekends
• limited wall space for hanging artwork

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Hi Mr. President, glad to talk to you again! Deciding between these exhibition venues is certainly a tough choice; nevertheless, it’s clear that the University Main Hall provides a more advantageous option for our student body.

First and foremost, the hall's 24/7 access is vital for our busy students who are often engrossed in their studies during the day. Given that the Community Center is only open on weekends, it would limit our audience significantly and likely cause utter confusion during peak hours.

Furthermore, although the Main Hall is slightly more pricey at $200, its vast space and natural lighting will ensure the artwork looks stellar. By the same token, the Community Center’s basic lighting and limited wall space might make our final exhibition appear substandard. Choosing the Hall will allow us to sort out a much more professional display.

Do you think my points make sense? Let me know your thoughts!
