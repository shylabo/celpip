# Question

You work at a local grocery store and are responsible for selecting fruit baskets to feature in the front display. You have two options to choose from. Decide which one you prefer and in the next section, persuade your manager that your choice is the better option.

1. Discounted Fruit Basket
   • Includes apples, bananas, and pears
   • Some fruits are slightly overripe
   • Attractively priced at a deep discount
   • Great for quick sale and reducing waste

2. Standard Fruit Mix
   • Mixed imported fruits
   • Regular pricing
   • Long shelf life
   • No particular marketing appeal

Your manager suggests another option. Persuade them that your choice is better by comparing it to their suggestion.

Manager’s Choice:
Premium Organic Basket
• Includes seasonal, organic local produce
• Fresh and visually appealing
• Higher price point
• Appeals to health-conscious customers

Recording time: 60s

# Answer

Hi [Manager's Name], glad to talk to you again! Deciding between these fruit displays is certainly a tough choice; nevertheless, it’s clear that the Discounted Fruit Basket provides a more advantageous option for us today.
First and foremost, this approach is vital for reducing waste. Given that some of these items are starting to show signs of decay, we must act swiftly to ensure they remain valuable to our customers. If we stay on the ball with this aggressive pricing, clearing our inventory will be a piece of cake.
Furthermore, although your suggested Premium Organic Basket is visually astounding, its higher price point might not appeal to shoppers looking to cut back on their daily expenses. By the same token, it's more efficient to prioritize a quick sale of our current stock rather than holding off and risking more waste.
